Das Boot

Over the Summer I read Das Boot translated to English. This is a German book about a man's time in the German Navy on a U-boat during WW2. Lothar-Günther Buchheim wrote the book in 1973. The book is about one of the times he was out to sea on a German U-boat in the Atlantic. It talks about the U-boats, German Naval Strategy, and life on the U-boats through the author's experiences.

Accordion

When I went through my basement I found my Accordion that I got when I was a kid. I never really played it I just wanted it because it looked cool. The basic form of the Accordion is believed to have been invented by Christian Friedrich Ludwig Buschmann in Berlin in 1822. The earliest known simple Accordions were made by Timofey Vorontsov in Tula, Russia in 1820 but this is poorly documented. The Accordion was introduced to many other countries by Germans throughout the years.

New Shoes

Over the Summer I had to get new shoes. I went with some Adidas basketball shoes. Adidas is a German shoe company founded in 1949 in Herzogenaurach, Germany. Slaughterhouse-Five, or The Children's Crusade: A Duty-Dance with Death

For summer reading I read the book Slaughterhouse-Five, or The Children's Crusade: A Duty-Dance with Death by Kurt Vonnegut. This is an anti-war, Science fiction book that focuses mostly on the firebombing of Dresden in WW2. Kurt Vonnegut was an American soldier who fought the Nazi's. He was captured by them and later witnessed the firebombing of Dresden. On February 13th 1945 the USAAF and RAF began firebombing the German city of Dresden. The attack stopped on the 15th. The strategic targets were destroyed and the city was reduced to rubble. It is said the people suffocated to death due to the lack of oxygen from the large fires. It is also stated that the fires were hot enough to form glass from sand and other materials. 22,700–25,000 people died.
